Blantyre Secondary School proudly celebrates the achievement of Blessings Phambala, a Form Three student who emerged as the champion of the inaugural Blantyre City Public Speaking Competition for Secondary Schools.

The final event took place at Golden Peacock Hotel, where Blessings outshone 14 contestants from 9 schools. She clinched the 1st place prize of MWK 500,000. The runner-up, Eve Kaime from Swema Private Secondary School, and third-placed Fly Kantefa from Jacaranda Secondary School received MWK 300,000 and MWK 200,000 respectively.

Organized by the Centre for Governance and Leadership (Cegol) in partnership with several stakeholders including MUBAS Story Lab, the National Planning Commission, Golden Peacock Hotel, Kwera Fund, Maheu Beverages, NBS Bank, and the Anti-Corruption Bureau (ACB), the competition aimed to empower youth to influence Malawi‚Äôs development agenda.
