Blantyre Secondary School student Blessings Phambala has emerged the winner of the inaugural Blantyre City Public Speaking Competition for Secondary Schools after the grand finale at Golden Peacock Hotel in Blantyre.

The 16-year-old Form Three student outshined 14 other contestants from nine schools, including her runner-up Eve Kaime from Swema Private Secondary School and third-placed Fly Kantefa from Jacaranda Secondary School.

For her remarkable victory, Blessings received K500,000, while Kaime and Kantefa were awarded K300,000 and K200,000 respectively.

Chimwemwe Kaonga, team leader for the Center for Governance and Leadership (Cegol), said the competition gave youth a platform to shape the national development agenda.

The event was organized by Cegol in collaboration with MUBAS Story Lab, the National Planning Commission, Golden Peacock Hotel, Kwera Fund, Maheu Beverages, NBS Bank, and the Anti-Corruption Bureau (ACB). Guest of honour was Blantyre City Mayor Joseph Makwinja.
